Case study · Pedal EV chassis

Building a pedal EV chassis supply chain from India to Germany

In 2022, I partnered with a German start-up to industrialise and export a complete pedal EV chassis from India. They had a design and a vision – we built the supply chain.

  • Converted 3D concepts into 2D manufacturing drawings and documentation
  • Sourced and onboarded vendors for casting, machining, and sheet metal
  • Set up central assembly and shipped CKD kits to Germany
  • Coordinated production, quality and export logistics end-to-end

Result: Export-ready chassis assemblies with a repeatable, scalable supply chain from India to Germany.

Case study · Precision components

Scaling precision components for a global drive manufacturer

For a leading drive manufacturer with multiple plants in India, I helped standardise the supply of closing caps and spacer tubes across facilities.

  • Managed production of 30+ variants of closing caps and spacer tubes
  • Supplied multiple plants from a single, aligned vendor ecosystem
  • Stabilised quality, documentation and delivery performance

Result: A stable, multi-plant supply chain with consistent quality and on-time deliveries over 1.5–2 years.

Case study · Instrumentation castings

Improving cost & quality for high-value casting orders

For a key instrumentation client, I worked on casting projects with strict tolerance and reliability requirements.

  • Broke down part cost into material, machining, overheads & tooling
  • Worked with vendors to reduce rework and scrap
  • Protected margins while keeping the client cost-competitive

Result: Better profitability without compromising precision or export timelines.

How I work

How I like to run programmes

I’m practical, data-driven and not shy about hard conversations when quality or timelines slip.

No drama, just clarity

Problems aren’t villains in a twist ending—they’re signals. I surface risks early, map out options, and make sure everyone knows what’s coming before it hits the schedule or the margin.

Decisions built on reality, not optimism

Pricing, lead time, capacity, constraints—everything gets quantified. My plans aren’t based on how things should work, but how they actually work on the shop floor, in the vendor’s machine room, and in the customer’s calendar.

I translate between worlds.

I translate between worlds European OEM expectations vs Indian vendor realities—two different planets. I make them speak the same language, align incentives, and keep both sides moving without friction, drama, or excuses.

Shop floor minded

I’d rather sit with the machinist and solve the root cause than write a beautiful RCA that changes nothing.

Execution is the only metric that counts.

Fancy presentations don’t move containers. What matters is what leaves the warehouse: in spec, on time, at the cost we promised. My work is measured in shipments, not slides.

“Good manufacturing is just good conversations, documented well and executed on time.” – How I like to run work
